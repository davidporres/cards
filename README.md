I made Egyptian Ratscrew (ERS) for 2 players.
For those who don't know the rule, they are as follows:
  1. Both players start with 26 cards (half of the deck)
  2. The goal is to collect all of the cards
  3. Each player plays one card at a time (with the deck facing down) until 1 of 2 things happens
-->
  1. There is a "slap"
  2. This can happen when:
      2 cards of the same value are played in a row (e.g. 7, 7)
      There's a "sandwich" -- a card is placed in between two cards of the same value (e.g. 7, 2, 7)
      There's a staircase/reverse staircase -- 3 cards of increasing/decreasing value are played in a row (e.g. 7, 8, 9 / 9, 8, 7)
  3. When either of those happens, both players race to slap the pile
  4. Whoever slaps first gets the whole pile and puts it underneath their card pile
-->
  1. A face card is played (J, Q, K, A)
  2. In this case, whoever played the card will automatically get the pile unless 1 of 2 things happens
       The other player plays another face card, in which case this process will start again
       A "slap" instance shows up, and then whoever slaps gets the pile
     Each face card gives the other person a different amount of turns (they play cards x amount of times) to have one of those happen
     J = 1 turn -- they play one card and if nothing happens, the person who played the J wins the pile
     Q = 2 turns -- same thing, but they play 2 cards
     K = 3 turns
     A = 4 turns
Whoever wins the pile plays the first card of the next round, and it continues until 1 player has all of the cards.
Also, memorizing the card order is/can be a part of the game, so don't shuffle the cards mid-game.
For Player 1, the inputs are Q to play a card and A to slap.
For Player 2, the inputs are P to play a card and A to slap.
If you slap when you're not supposed to, you have to "burn" a card, putting the top card of your pile into the bottom of the middle pile.
